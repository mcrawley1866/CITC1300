Season One

*Spoiler Alert*

Episode 1: Wildcat is Down

In Episode 1, we meet Secret Service Agent Xavier Collins, who is raising two kids in what appears to be a peaceful community. That peace is shattered when President Cal Bradford is found murdered. Xavier begins investigating but quickly becomes a suspect himself, especially given his rocky history with the president. Clues at the scene—like a missing tablet and a cryptic cigarette—point to an inside job. Flashbacks reveal political tension and personal loss. The episode ends with a twist: Paradise is actually a high-tech underground bunker built after a global catastrophe.

Episode 2: Sinatra

In Episode 2, flashbacks reveal how tech mogul Samantha Redmond—codenamed "Sinatra"—created Paradise as a secret underground refuge. In the present, Xavier is interrogated as a murder suspect but is ultimately cleared by Dr. Torabi, who believes he didn’t kill the president. Vice President Baines takes over leadership, while Xavier grows suspicious that Sinatra may be hiding more than she lets on.

Episode 3: The Architect

In Episode 3, Xavier meets with Dr. Torabi, who reveals she helped choose Paradise’s residents—raising suspicions about why Xavier’s wife was left out. Their bond deepens as trust issues grow. Meanwhile, evidence surfaces that President Bradford died from blunt force trauma, and two agents come under scrutiny for disabling security. Xavier becomes increasingly suspicious of the leadership and the secrets they’re hiding.

Episode 4: Agent Billy Pace

In Episode 4, the episode delves into Billy's past, revealing his troubled childhood and mercenary work. Xavier uncovers that Billy was involved in a mission to eliminate scientists who discovered the surface is still habitable. When Billy threatens to expose these secrets, his girlfriend, Agent Jane Driscoll, poisons him, silencing him for good and revealing the dangerous extent of the leadership’s control.

Episode 5: In the Palaces of Crowned Kings

In Episode 5 of Paradise, the narrative delves into President Cal Bradford's final day, offering insights into his strained relationships and his pursuit of truth before his untimely death. The episode begins with Cal's attempts to connect with his father, Kane, who suffers from dementia, highlighting unresolved family tensions. Cal's investigation into surface expeditions reveals discrepancies in official reports, suggesting a cover-up by the elite, including Samantha "Sinatra" Redmond. Before his demise, Cal hides crucial evidence in a library, aiming to expose the truth. The episode concludes with a chilling message displayed on the bunker’s sky screen: "They're lying to you," deepening the mystery and setting the stage for ensuing conflicts.

Episode 6: You Asked for Miracles

img

In Episode 6, Xavier and his allies work to expose the truth about Paradise by using the sky-dome system to broadcast their message. Sinatra retaliates by taking Xavier's daughter, Presley, hostage and offers him a chance to reunite with his presumed-dead wife, Teri, if he complies. This revelation reignites Xavier's determination to challenge Sinatra’s control and uncover the truth, setting the stage for the season finale.

Episode 7: The Day

img

In Episode 7 of Paradise, the series unveils the catastrophic event that led to the collapse of civilization. A supervolcano eruption beneath Antarctica triggers a global megatsunami, causing widespread devastation. Amidst the chaos, President Cal Bradford activates a secret electromagnetic pulse (EMP) network to disable nuclear weapons, aiming to prevent further destruction. This decision, however, creates a rift between Bradford and Secret Service agent Xavier Collins, who had been misled about his wife Teri's fate. In the present day, Samantha "Sinatra" Redmond reveals that the EMP preserved Atlanta, where Teri survived, and demands that Xavier capture the true assassin of President Bradford to reunite with his family.

Episode 8: The Man Who Kept the Secrets

In the Season 1 finale of Paradise, the identity of President Cal Bradford’s assassin is revealed. The killer is Trent, the bunker’s librarian, who was once a project manager during the facility’s construction. Twelve years earlier, Trent discovered toxic conditions at the construction site that led to worker illnesses and deaths. After being fired and failing to gain media attention, he attempted to assassinate President Bradford but was thwarted by Xavier Collins. Disguised as the librarian, Trent infiltrated the bunker, ultimately killing Bradford with a construction tool. After confessing, Trent commits suicide in front of Collins and Agent Robinson. In the aftermath, Xavier confronts Samantha “Sinatra” Redmond, demanding the release of his daughter, Presley. Before Redmond can respond, Agent Jane Driscoll arrives and shoots Redmond non-lethally. The season concludes with Xavier preparing to leave the bunker to search for his presumed-alive wife, Teri, using Bradford’s notes as a guide.
